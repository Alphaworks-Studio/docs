---
title: "Repair your first model"
description: "Fix common 3D printing issues automatically"
icon: "wand-magic-sparkles"
---

The repair process automatically fixes common printing issues — holes, thin walls, non-printable geometry — and optimizes your model for your printer.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Repair">
    From your model card on the home page, or from the model detail page, click the **Repair** button. The repair dialog opens.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/getting-started/repair-modal.png" alt="Repair dialog with model type selection and settings" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a model type">
    Select the preset that best matches your model:
    - **Character** — Figurines, characters, organic shapes
    - **Mechanical** — Engineering parts, brackets, functional prints
    - **Vehicle** — Cars, aircraft, vehicles
    - **Custom** — No preset, full control over settings
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the scale">
    Choose how to size your model:
    - **Target dimension** — Set the longest side to a specific size (e.g., 6 inches)
    - **Multiplier** — Scale by a factor (e.g., 2x to double the size)
    - **Don't scale** — Keep the original dimensions
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a quality level">
    - **Fast** — Quick preview, lower detail (~30 seconds)
    - **Standard** — Balanced quality and speed (1–3 minutes)
    - **Detailed** — Maximum detail, longer processing (3–10 minutes)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the repair">
    Click **Start Repair**. A progress overlay shows the current stage and completion percentage.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/getting-started/repair-progress.png" alt="Repair progress overlay showing current stage" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

When the repair completes, you're taken to the repair detail page where you can view the fix report, compare before and after, and download your print-ready model.

<Tip>
  Not sure which preset to use? Start with **Custom** — it works well for most models. You can always re-repair with different settings.
</Tip>

<Note>
  Your first **2 repairs are free**. After that, you need a subscription to continue repairing. See [Plans and pricing](/account/plans-and-pricing).
</Note>

The AI Assistant is a built-in chat assistant that can see your model, understand its properties, and help you prepare it for printing. It can answer questions, suggest fixes, and even run tools for you.

## Opening the AI Assistant

Click the **sparkle icon** at the bottom right of the editor toolbar. The chat panel slides open on the right side of the screen.

## What it can see

When you start a conversation, the AI Assistant automatically has access to:
- Your model's dimensions, polygon count, and volume
- Analysis results (if available) — detected issues, printability score
- 360° thumbnail images of your model from multiple angles
- The current state of your editor settings

This means you can ask about your model directly — no need to describe it.

## How to use it

Type a message in the input box at the bottom and press **Enter** or click **Send**. The assistant responds in real time, and you can have a multi-turn conversation.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/editor/ai/quick-prompts.png" alt="Quick prompt suggestions in the AI Assistant" />
</Frame>

**Quick prompts** — When you first open the chat, you'll see suggested prompts to get started:
- "Analyze this model for printability"
- "What should I do to prepare this for printing?"
- "Are there any issues with this model?"

Click any of these to send it immediately.

## Tool calls

The AI Assistant can run tools on your model as part of the conversation. When it does, you'll see a **tool call card** in the chat showing:
- Which tool is being used
- The parameters it chose
- The result when it completes

You can expand these cards to see the details.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/editor/ai/tool-call.png" alt="Tool call card showing AI executing an operation" />
</Frame>

## Managing conversations

- **Stop** — Click the red stop button to interrupt a response
- **Clear** — Click the new conversation button in the header to start fresh
- Conversations are saved automatically so you can pick up where you left off

<Tip>
  The AI Assistant is great for getting a quick printability assessment. Just ask "Is this model ready to print?" and it will analyze and give you specific recommendations.
</Tip>

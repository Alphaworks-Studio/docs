Hollow creates an empty interior inside your model, leaving only a shell of the specified thickness. This dramatically reduces material usage, printing cost, and weight.

## When to use this

For large models where a solid print would waste material, or when you want to reduce weight without changing the outer appearance.

## Settings

### Shell thickness (0–20 mm)
How thick the outer walls should be. Set to **0** to keep the model solid (off). **Default: 0 (off).**

## Step by step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the Hollow tool">
    Click **Hollow** in the toolbar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set shell thickness">
    Enter a value in mm. 2–3 mm works well for most full-color printers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a repair">
    Hollowing is applied during the repair process.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips

- **2–3 mm** shell thickness is typical for full-color printers
- Make sure the shell is at least as thick as the [Wall Thickness](/editor/tools/thickness) setting
- Use the [Slice View](/editor/tools/slice-view) tool after repair to verify the hollow interior looks correct

<Warning>
  Very thin shells (under 1 mm) may not be strong enough to hold their shape after printing.
</Warning>

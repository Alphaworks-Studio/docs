Choose a quality level that matches your needs. You can always re-repair at a different level.

| Level | Processing time | Detail | Best for |
|-------|----------------|--------|----------|
| **Fast** | ~30 seconds | Lower detail, smaller file | Quick previews, checking printability |
| **Standard** | 1–3 minutes | Balanced detail and speed | Most prints, everyday use |
| **Detailed** | 3–10 minutes | Maximum detail, larger file | High-resolution printers, presentation models |

## What changes between levels

- **Resolution** — Higher quality uses a finer internal processing grid, capturing smaller features
- **Polygon count** — Higher quality produces more polygons for smoother surfaces
- **Detail preservation** — Higher quality is more aggressive about keeping small features intact

<Tip>
  Start with **Standard** for your first repair. If you need more detail, re-repair with **Detailed**. Use **Fast** for quick test runs when you're experimenting with settings.
</Tip>

<Note>
  Processing time varies based on model complexity. A simple shape repairs in seconds even at Detailed quality; a complex character with textures takes longer.
</Note>

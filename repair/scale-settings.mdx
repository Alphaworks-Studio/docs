---
title: "Scale settings"
description: "Resize your model to the exact print dimensions you need"
icon: "up-right-and-down-left-from-center"
---

Scale settings let you resize your model during repair. Choose from three modes:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Target dimension">
    Set the longest side of your model to a specific size. For example, "6 inches tall" or "150 mm wide".

    Choose your unit: **millimeters (mm)**, **centimeters (cm)**, or **inches**.

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/repair/scale-target.png" alt="Scale settings with target dimension mode" />
    </Frame>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Multiplier">
    Scale by a factor. For example:
    - **2x** doubles the size
    - **0.5x** halves it
    - **1x** keeps the same size

    <Frame>
      <img src="/images/repair/scale-multiplier.png" alt="Scale settings with multiplier mode" />
    </Frame>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Don't scale">
    Keep the model at its original dimensions. Use this when your model is already at the correct size.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
  Double-check your scale settings before repairing. An incorrectly scaled model wastes printing material and time.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  If you're printing on a Mimaki full-color printer, the typical maximum build size is about **10 inches (254 mm)** on the longest side.
</Tip>

After every repair, our AI generates a detailed report explaining what issues were found and how they were resolved.

  <img src="/images/repair/fix-report.png" alt="AI fix report showing issue categories and fixes" />
</Frame>

## Report sections

### Summary
A quick overview: how many issues were found, how many were fixed, and an overall printability assessment.

### Issue categories

Issues are grouped by severity:

| Severity | Color | Examples |
|----------|-------|---------|
| **Critical** | Red | Open holes, zero-thickness walls, non-sealed surfaces |
| **Medium** | Yellow | Thin walls near the minimum, tight clearances |
| **Minor** | Gray | Unnecessary geometry, suboptimal topology |

Each issue shows:
- The name of the issue
- A short description of what was found
- A **Fixed** pill when it was successfully resolved

### Improvements
A summary of all optimizations applied: polygon reduction, surface smoothing, texture cleanup, and more.

### Settings used
The exact settings used for this repair, so you can reproduce the result or adjust and re-repair.

## Before and after comparison

The repair detail page shows a **360° image preview** you can drag to rotate the model. To compare the original and repaired versions, click the **Open in 3D Viewer** button — it opens the editor's side-by-side split view, with the original model on the left and the repaired version on the right.

  <img src="/images/repair/before-after.png" alt="Side-by-side comparison of original and repaired model" />

<Tip>
  If the report shows unfixed issues, try re-repairing with different settings. For example, increasing wall thickness often resolves remaining thin-wall warnings.
</Tip>

<Note>
  The AI report is generated automatically and focuses on print-readiness. It's designed to help you understand the repairs without needing 3D printing expertise.
</Note>

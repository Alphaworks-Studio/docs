## Watching the progress

After you click **Start Repair**, a full-screen progress overlay appears.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/getting-started/repair-progress.png" alt="Repair progress overlay showing current stage and progress bar" />
</Frame>

You'll see:
- A **progress bar** from 0% to 100%
- The **current stage name** (e.g., "Analyzing Geometry", "Applying Fixes")
- A **description** of what's happening

## Canceling a repair

Click the **Cancel** button at any time to stop the repair. Your original model is never modified — you can always start over with different settings.

## When the repair completes

You're automatically taken to the **repair detail page** where you can:
- View the [AI fix report](/repair/ai-fix-report) — what was found and what was fixed
- Compare original and repaired models [side by side](/editor/3d-viewer)
- [Download](/getting-started/download-results) the result in your preferred format
- Open the repaired model in the [3D editor](/editor/overview) for further modifications

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/getting-started/repair-complete.png" alt="Completed repair detail page with fix report" />
</Frame>

## If a repair fails

Occasionally a repair may not complete successfully. If this happens:
- Try a lower [quality level](/repair/quality-levels) (Fast instead of Detailed)
- Try a different [model type preset](/repair/model-types)
- Check if your model file is corrupted by opening it in another 3D viewer
- See [Troubleshooting](/help/troubleshooting) for more solutions

<Note>
  Your original model is always preserved. Repairs create a new copy — they never modify your upload.
</Note>
